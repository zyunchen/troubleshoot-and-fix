Why Won't My New Dishwasher Drain?

Recently, a regular customer asked me about dishwasher installation pricing. Since they're a repeat customer, I offered a discounted rate of $50. However, they mentioned they had already installed it themselves but were having drainage issues. I immediately knew what the problem was and provided a quick solution over the phone - free of charge!

The Common Problem

When installing a new dishwasher, the installation process itself isn't complicated - there are plenty of online tutorials available. However, the drainage connection is where most people run into trouble, especially when connecting to a garbage disposal.

Diagram showing the circled area where the knockout plug needs to be removed

The Solution

The issue is that new garbage disposals come with a sealed knockout plug in the dishwasher connection port (the circled area in the diagram). This plug must be removed before connecting your dishwasher's drain hose.

Step-by-Step Fix

1. Turn off the garbage disposal and unplug it for safety

2. Locate the dishwasher connection port on your garbage disposal

3. Use a screwdriver or hammer to carefully remove the knockout plug

4. Connect your dishwasher's drain hose to the now-open port

5. Secure the connection with a hose clamp

Pro Tip

Always check if the knockout plug has been removed before installing a new dishwasher. This simple step can save you hours of troubleshooting!
